Forest Governance and Administration Document Archive

Timeline of documents pertaining to the acquisition of the Lange Forest

Date            Description
December 1973 Land and Water Conservation Fund (LWCF) Grants-in-Aid Manual including Compliance Requirements
January 25, 1978 Heritage Conservation and Recreation Service (HCRS) Program Goals
March 7, 1978 The Town vote to direct the Selectboard to pursue the project
July 23, 1978 The Langes' offer of 770 acres for the HCRS program, as a memorial to their son
August 8, 1978 VT Forest, Parks and Recreation (FPR) letter to Langes re: Title 6 & Outdoor Recreation Requirements
August 21, 1978 Agenda for Public Informational Meeting to Lange Acquisition
August 21, 1978 Minutes of Public Informational Meeting on Lange Acquisition
January, 1979 Lange Property Soils and Environmental Survey
January, 1979 HCRS Program Narrative and Assurances
(Note: The copy of the Program Narrative and Assurances is undated but precedes 8/17/1979, the executed date of the Lange Contract. The Narrative and Assurances is listed on that approval as an included document.)
January 24, 1979 Lange Acquisition Project approved by the State of Vermont
February 16, 1979 HRCS Grant Administrator cautions Town Clerk about logging
February 28, 1979 HRCS Grant Administrator cautions SB Chair Sargent about logging
March 5, 1979HCRS Grant Administrator advises SB Member Gatto about long-term grant responsibilities advising Selectboard Member Gatto about longterm responsibility
March 14, 1979Certification of Town Vote, 92-53 to approve Lange Acquisition Project
May 22, 1979HCRS General Provisions
May 23, 1979HCRS Recreation and Forest Management Plan
June 11, 1979 VT Agency of Transportation (AOT) Opinion regarding Bald Top Rd as possible year-round access
June 12, 1979 Surveyor's Summary of the 3 access roads
Map for HCRS Access #1, via Brushwood Road
Map for HCRS Access #2, via Bald Top Road
Map for HCRS Access #3, via Knox Road
July 20, 1979 HCRS Assurance of Compliance with Title 6 Requirements
July 23, 1979 HCRS Performance Agreement with the State of Vermont
August 17, 1979 HCRS Officially Executes the LWCF Contract; copies of the completed contract and related documents sent to the Town of Fairlee
August 28, 1979HCRS informs the State of VT that the Fairlee Acquisition (Project #50-00320) has been approved
October 23, 1979VT Forests, Parks and Recreation (FPR) Dept. discusses need for year-round access with SB Chair Sargent
December 7, 1979 Town Attorney Otterman advises SB Chair Sargent regarding HCRS long-term obligations
January 3, 1980 Lease with Aloha Foundation for parking area at Brushwood Rd
January 3, 1980 Insurance Underwriter concerned about coverage for snowmobiles
January 4, 1980HCRS Grant Administrator advises SB Chair Sargent regarding federal regulations governing use of HCRS-funded property
January 5, 1980Fairlee Selectmen outline a plan to avoid the “burden” of HCRS obligations
January 28, 1980 The Kee Committee rejects SB attempt to override the will of the voters
Same as above, but retyped for readability
January 30, 1980The Kee Committee reviews actions completed to date and outlines ideas for managing the acquisition. [The governance structure — an “elected by the voters” Recreational Board — for the HCRS Contract had been formally executed by the Town, the State of Vermont and the U.S. Department of the Interior earlier, on August 28, 1979]
January 30, 1980Town's Insurance Agency Discusses Workers' Comp and Liability Insurance with SB Chair Sargent
February 19, 1980County Forester responds to SB regarding the potential for marketable timber on the Lange property
February 19, 1980Excerpt from the LWCF Forest Management Plan Handwritten notes show that someone in the Town Hall refers to p.7 of the HCRS Recreation and Forest Management Plan “as submitted and approved.” This had occurred 6 months prior, on August 17, 1979.
March 4, 1980 SB explains delay in accepting the deed from the Langes
March 10, 1980 SB creates an appointed Lange Forest Board, contrary to the HCRS’ specified “elected by the voters” Recreation Board: "Within the next year the interim Kee Committee will hand over management of the HCRS project to a permanent Board of Trustees of at least 5 members serving for a term of 5 years and elected by the town.” (See HCRS Management Plan of May 23, 1979)
March 15, 1980Fairlee SB Offers to purchase the Lange property at full cost of $158,500 without voter approval or notice of the proposed expenditure
March 17, 1980The Langes reject the SB's March 15th offer, insisting they wish to donate their property, as a memorial to their son
March 17, 1980 Deed for the Lange HCRS-acquired Land is Recorded in the Fairlee Town Land Records
March 17, 1980 Map for the Lange HCRS-acquired Land is recorded in the Farlee Town Map Book 2
March 17, 1980 The Fairlee SB discusses appointing the first 5 members to the Lange Forest Board, created 1 week earlier as an appointed board, contrary to the HCRS’ specified “elected by the voters” Boards
March 17, 1980 SB Crafts a “Proclamation" from its motion of a week earlier, creating and authorizing an appointed Lange Forest Board. This Proclamation will be published in the Agenda for the next year's Town Meeting entitled, “RULES AND REGULATIONS OF FAIRLEE HERITAGE AND RECREATION SERVICE PROJECT (LANGE FOREST)"
April 23, 1980The SB officially appoints the first 5 members of the Lange Forest Board
March 3, 1981The Town Treasurer's Report includes the SB's Proclamation, "RULES and REGULATIONS ..." of March 17, 1980
